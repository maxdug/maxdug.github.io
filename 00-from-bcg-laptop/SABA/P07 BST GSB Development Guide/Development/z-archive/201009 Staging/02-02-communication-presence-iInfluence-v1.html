Communication, Presence, and Influence
On-The-Job
  • Before every meeting, call, or email, take some time to define your key message and goal of the meeting/email; write down the one or two main objectives on paper so you have it in front of you
  • Apply the pyramid principle to start with the key message first
  • Before communicating to your stakeholders, think about the potential questions that they may have and try to address them beforehand
  • Think about who is the recipient of your message? What are their drivers? What does their daily schedule look like? Tailor your message accordingly
  • Take any opportunities to speak up at meetings; take the lead on preparing a deck for a meeting
  • Be courageous and present at internal meetings/calls
  • Not only wait for your turn to speak but listen actively to others’ point of view
  • During a presentation/call/meeting make sure all participants’ contribution link to the key messages, stress only key ideas, and prepare supporting evidence if needed
  • Take some time for reflection: Look back at some of your own written communication and ask yourself how it could be made clearer and more concise
Coaching, Mentoring, & Feedback
  • Practice and dry-run verbal presentations several times beforehand; ask a colleague to give you feedback
  • Ask someone you trust to review your written work before submitting it
  • Ask stakeholders or peers for ideas and feedback, listen to them open-minded, and act on suggestions
