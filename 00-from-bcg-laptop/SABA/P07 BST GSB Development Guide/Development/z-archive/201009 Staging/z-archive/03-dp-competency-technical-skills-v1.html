Technical Skills & Expertise
Experience
  • Identify your strengths and any key gaps by talking to someone who has deep expertise in your area
  • Research an area of interest and share back to your colleagues
  • Reserve regular space in the week to review magazines and articles; reserve time for on-demand training modules (LAB, LinkedIn Learning)
  • Complete your research by reading BCG Perspectives and other BCG publications
  • Grow your expertise on relevant subjects, feeling more confident and comfortable when providing relevant information, advice and suggesting action points
Exposure
  • Reach out to BCG experts to discuss topics of interest
  • Build a network of colleagues who share similar interests or roles
  • Join external industry/topic networks as well as conferences.
  • Get involved in a demanding project, where you will have to use and update your functional/technical skills and knowledge
  • Increase your expertise by going deeper when you are asked about a valuable topic for you or your area, that may be used in the future again: schedule some more time to read more and store the relevant documents/publications
  • Summarize your findings in a short executive summary and “So What” for yourself, store it in your files
Education
  • Learn about key software (full topic, see subtopics for applications)
  • PowerPoint
  • Excel
  • Digital Teaming Suite (Slack, Egnyte, Trello)
  • Advanced Analytics Tools (Alteryx and Tableau)
  • Agile
  • Stay up to date with your technical/functional area of expertise using LinkedIn Learning, blogs, industry sites, BCG Perspectives, other internal and external materials
