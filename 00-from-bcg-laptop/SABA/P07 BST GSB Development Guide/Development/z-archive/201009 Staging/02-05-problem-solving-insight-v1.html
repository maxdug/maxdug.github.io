Problem Solving & Insight
On-The-Job
  • Try to make a big/complex task as simple as possible
  • Conduct complex and rigorous analysis by building a work plan at the beginning: list working steps, issues, hypotheses, analyses, and required information.
  • List the data items you require and determine the potential resources to leverage
  • Identify and collect relevant data efficiently, prioritizing data collection to focus on most relevant information first
  • Set a clear deadline for data collection, and at the deadline, review your data collection
  • Ask yourself: Are data telling enough to answer the initial question? Can I apply the 80/20 rule? What is the ‘So What’ of my findings?
  • Proactively perform intensive data validation checks on your main results; review it throughout the process, e.g., at every end of day
  • Document your steps appropriate documentation
  • Rely less on others' quality control and implement more stringent self-checks
  • Show your analysis to a colleague for double-checking; explaining to others will often reveal contradictions and gaps
Coaching, Mentoring, & Feedback
  • Seek input from colleagues/team manager for their ideas or previous experience in solving similar types of problems or decision making
  • Sit with a colleague or your team and do some brainstorming. Also allow unusual or unrealistic ideas in the first place — they can be sorted out later
