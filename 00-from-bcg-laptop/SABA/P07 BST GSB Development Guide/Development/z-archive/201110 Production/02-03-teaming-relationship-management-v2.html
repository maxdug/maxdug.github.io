Teaming & Relationship Management Be a trusted colleague to peers, team members, and stakeholders
On-The-Job
  • Be a trusted colleague to peers, team members, and stakeholders by being respectful and open-minded
  • Build strong relationships at all levels with customers and stakeholders
  • Increase your emotional intelligence; look at the big picture and put yourself in your colleagues/stakeholders’ shoes before making assumptions
  • Dedicate a specific amount of time per week to developing relationships; block time in your Outlook calendar for that purpose
  • Be a team player; offer your help where it could be needed and not only within your own team: expand your interest in the BCG community and become a known person for being reliable, integrated, and interested on other´s well-being
  • Take time for reflection: Compare and contrast successful and difficult customer interactions
  • Have a good communication flow with your customers, set clear expectations
  • Be flexible and adapt your approach when needed: actively listen to your customers and put yourself in their shoes
  • List all the different groups of customers you have. Determine for each stakeholder their expectations and define action steps how you will meet them
  • Think if you would need anything from their side to achieve even better results, and ask them for it
  • Invest interest and time understanding your customer and ask yourself: may they be having a difficult day? How can I help them? What do they expect from me?
Coaching, Mentoring, & Feedback
  • Catch up with your key working relationships regularly; ask them proactively about their concerns, ideas, and inputs
  • Proactively reach out to team members for a formal or informal chat When reaching out, consider your stakeholders’ daily schedule and situation (e.g., stressful project)
  • Use informal conversations at lunch or when having a coffee not only to show your interest in your colleagues’ daily work, but also in their hobbies, concerns, difficulties, and aspirations: there’s always common points where you can help and support each other
  • Be or become a buddy or a mentor
  • Actively participate in team calls/meetings
  • Talk to peers/colleagues who have worked with your key customers/stakeholders: Brainstorm actions to position yourself better with them; discuss with your manager and ask for support
  • Volunteer for office roles/committees (office events, social impact, etc.)
  • Get involved in activities outside work which involve teamwork, e.g., team sports, local
