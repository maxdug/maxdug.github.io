People Development & Leadership
On-The-Job
  • Build, bond and deploy your team effectively, keeping the team members aware of the overall agenda and expectations
    • Create a skill map of your topic/area and think where you can apply your team members’ specific skill-set best
    • Keep the team aware of accountabilities, changes, progress, and findings
    • Communicate expectations clearly and provide timely feedback
  • Invest in individual development, acting as a coach when needed.
    • Reserve a fixed time per week/bi-weekly to speak to your team members (30 min to 1 hour)
    • Regularly provide feedback on relevant areas timely (feedback is always best served 'fresh')
    • Actively listen to your team members
    • What is their situation?
    • What are their successes? - Praise them
    • What challenges do they face? – What support do they need from you?
    • Think about what motivates and engages your team members. Try to emphasize and support these activities/behaviors
  • Maintain a balance between delegation and control.
  • Generate enthusiasm. Be available and approachable to the team members.
Coaching, Mentoring, & Feedback
  • Openly discuss with your team members their preferred style of management
  • Meet from time to time to recap your charter, and ask for feedback as well as provide it
  • Organize regular team catch-ups
  • Integrate "update rounds" where everyone reports about current tasks, challenges, and successes as well as informal information (e.g., weddings, birthdays, vacation; if appropriate)
  • Organize team events/dinners or working lunches/coffee breaks in-person or over Zoom
