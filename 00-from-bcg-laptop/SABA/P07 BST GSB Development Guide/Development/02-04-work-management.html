Work Management Build a work plan at the beginning of your tasks
On-The-Job
  • Build a work plan at the beginning of the task: list working steps, issues, hypotheses, analyses, and required information. Consider what elements of that project could go wrong and possible workarounds
  • Before starting a task, take the time to check if it makes sense in the light of the overall plan
  • Prioritize your work well; find the right balance between important and urgent tasks; de-prioritize tasks that are neither important nor urgent (see Eisenhower prioritization matrix); conduct this exercise every morning when starting work and every evening before leaving
  • Challenge yourself to approach problems creatively, use ideations tools (e.g., mind mapping) to brainstorm different perspectives on your task/project
Coaching, Mentoring, & Feedback
  • Talk through a current or past project plan with your Line Manager or trusted colleague. Discuss your approach and where you did well and where you could have done better. Listen open-minded, so you can obtain ideas to improve
  • Be curious what other people do; ask someone you see managing his/her work very well about their tools and tricks
