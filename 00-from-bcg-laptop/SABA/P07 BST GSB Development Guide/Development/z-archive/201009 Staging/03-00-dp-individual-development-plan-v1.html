Individual Development Plans (IDP)
IDPs are used to build an increased level of ability in your current role and/or to help you advance in your career. While you will most likely think about this type of development when setting your annual goals, an IDP can be created at any time. Reviewing and revising your plan on a quarterly basis alongside your line manager is recommended.
  1. 1Ideally, an IDP is created with input from both the manager and the employee
  2. 2Research shows that about 70% of the actions taken to achieve development goals are through on-the-job experiences, 20% through mentoring/coaching and 10% through training
  3. 3An IDP should concentrate on ways to leverage strengths as well as improve areas where you are not as strong
  4. 4Focus on developing no more than one or two areas where you want/need to improve
  5. 5Your individual development goals must be achievable
  6. 6Include action steps that will assist you in achieving your individual development goals and include an implementation timetable
  7. 7Think about what success could look like, and how that could be measured
