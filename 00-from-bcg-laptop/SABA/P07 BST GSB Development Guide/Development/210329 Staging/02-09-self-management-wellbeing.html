Self Management and Wellbeing Act independently and take on challenges
On-The-Job
  • Act independently and take on challenges willingly, being proactive, and suggesting new ideas
  • Ask for help when needed, work on a team when it helps to meet better results and to meet deadlines
  • Express motivation, initiative, and enthusiasm, showing high energy levels and commitment to tasks
  • Seek and act on feedback for self-development
  • If you feel overloaded, try to step back: Are there tasks which are neither important nor urgent? Try to deprioritize them
  • Try to reduce the level of tension and relax even in challenging situations – it will help you to be even more effective and to reduce the stress you put on yourself
  • Create a "risk mitigation plan": write down all potential risks and challenges and think of ways of how to overcome them; think of whose support you may need
  • Try not to be overeager, take half an hour of your time per day to focus on something else. When you come back to the issue, think about how the individual pieces fit with the overall picture — it will help you to prioritize
Coaching, Mentoring, & Feedback
  • Talk openly to your Team Manager, and ask for support
  • Create a Development Plan together with your Team Manager; leverage provided materials
  • Manage your colleagues and stakeholders’ expectations since the beginning of the processes, this will help you to avoid future misunderstandings and workload will go more smoothly
  • Practice to say “no” and push back when needed with confidence and politeness, feeling comfortable and from a justified standpoint
  • Think of potential support options, e.g., other team members, other colleagues, assistants, students, third party providers: which tasks can be delegated to them?
  • Prioritize issues and highlight the most urgent ones to your Team Manager/ stakeholder, leveraging their input in getting to a solution
  • Leverage the Eisenhower prioritization matrix; it is a useful tool to identify the relevance and urgency of tasks
